Moose
A moose is the largest member of the deer family, easily recognized by its massive antlers and long legs. They inhabit cold northern forests near lakes and rivers. Moose are generally calm but can be extremely dangerous if threatened. Their great size and unique appearance make them iconic animals of North America and northern Europe.

Elephant
An elephant is the largest land animal on Earth, known for its intelligence, strong memory, and complex social behavior. Its large ears help regulate body temperature, and its trunk is a versatile tool for eating, drinking, and communication. Elephants thrive in savannas and forests, living in matriarch-led herds. Their presence is vital to maintaining balance in their ecosystems.

Leopard
A leopard is a powerful and agile big cat known for its golden coat covered in dark rosettes. It is an expert climber and often drags its prey up trees for safety. Leopards are solitary hunters and thrive in forests, grasslands, and even mountains. Their stealth and strength make them one of the most successful predators in the wild.
